Week of 15 Jan - 21 Jan

First Blog post

Posted by Catherine Lu on January 20 2018

1.) What are your expectations of the class?

From this course, I expect to learn about C++, including all the little details of the language. Besides C++, I think Downing will also teach about important industy tools, like Docker. Hopefully by the end of this class, I can become proficient in both the language and tools. From what my friends speak about Downing, praising him for his teaching skills, I believe I can achieve my goals for this semester.

2.) What do you think of this week?

As far as first weeks go, I think this one went pretty well. We went over the syllbus and began an introduction on C++. I liked how Downing pointed out key differences between C++ and Java. It really helps in differentiating between the two languages. Then on Friday's class, we learned about the Collatz Conjecture, which certainly fascinated me.

3.) What's in your way?

Last semester, I took CS 439 Operating Systems, which destroyed me. Often I didn't have much time to pursue my hobbies or work on sideprojects. So this semester, I will be working on to relive my favorite activities or dusting off some forgotten code. Besides that and my classes, I expect this semester to be quite relaxed compared to the fall.

4.) What will you do next week?

Next week, I will start doing readings for all four of classes: algorithms, OOP, government, and history. Sadly, I am a slow reader, so hopefully I can keep up. For OOP specifically, I anticipate the first project will be released, so I will definitely be working on that. If not, I still need to do my first competitive programming assignment, which seems quite tricky. Then there's the daily quizes in OOP and government I need to review for. So far, the second week of class looks like a pretty standard college week. I guess this means my winter break is truly over.

6.) What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

My pick of the week is Bootstrap. It is a framework, used for front-end web development. If you want to make website, I think Boostrap offers a nice template to start off without overwhelming yourself. After using their code, you can really digest and learn how does HTML and front-end work. For those who learn programming languages through code examples, Bootstrap was really helpful for me. Plus, I got my own website at the end.

Here's a their "Getting Stared" Page. https://getbootstrap.com/docs/3.3/getting-started/

Then here's their "Components" page, which lists all the things you can do with Bootstrap. https://getbootstrap.com/docs/3.3/components/

I hope people find it as useful as I did when making a website.
