CS 371p Spring 2018: Catherine Lu

Week of 19 Feb - 25 Feb Post

Here's my sixth blog post.

1.) What did you do this past week?

The beginning of the week was heavily focused on the algo exam and Career Fair. Both were okay: could have prepared better. On the happier side of things, one of my friends wanted me to prepare him for LonghornRun, due to my background of running 5Ks, so I agreed to help. We started on Wednesday, and I have been killing him ever sense. Just kidding. I didn’t actually kill him, but I have been encouraging him to push himself more. Like all sports, long-distance running requires great discipline to get better. So far, he’s been doing a good job. Hopefully, he keeps it up.

2.) What's in your way?

So far this week has mostly been the same workload to do: school, officer stuff, side projects, and internship hunting. Though, I have been doing more interview prep, like practicing on leetcode and hackerrank. Thankfully, doing the competitive programing assignments gives extra practice. Another task I have been doing is creating a training regime for my above friend.

3.) What will you do next week?

Thankfully, most of my exams finished this or last week. So the only big thing I have is the OOP exam, which I will be studying for. Another big thing is doubling my interview prep, since interview season has basically started, especially with the recent Career Fair event. Hopefully, I can find internship this year.

4.) What’s my experience of the class?

So far I been doing better on quizzes, which is good. Though the material has been getting more complicated, which may be foreshadowing a drop in my quiz grades. Hopefully, this doesn’t happen. Regardless, this only means I need to spend more time reviewing the material after class and double-checking that I understand the concepts.

5.) What's my pick-of-the-week or tip-of-the-week?

My pick of the week is Leetcode. This website is similar to HackerRank, but I feel the problems are much harder, which is great for interviews. If you’re still looking for internships or tech jobs, I highly recommend this website to practice. It really challenges you. If Leetcode is not enough, maybe you could try some competitive programming websites, that I discovered from the taking the class: Codeforces, CodeWars, TopCoder, CodeChef, Project Euler and SPOJ. Though, these are more contest-oriented but still great practice.
